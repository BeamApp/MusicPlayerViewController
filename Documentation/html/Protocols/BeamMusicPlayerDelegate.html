BeamMusicPlayerDelegate Protocol Reference

Conforms to NSObject
Declared in BeamMusicPlayerDelegate.h

Overview

The Delegate of the BeamMusicPlayerViewController must adopt the BeamMusicPlayerDelegate protocol to track changes in the state of the music player.

Tasks

  • – playerDidStartPlaying:

    Called by the player after the player started playing a song.
  • – playerShouldStartPlaying:

    Called after a user presses the “play”-button but before the player actually starts playing. If the value returned is NO, the player won’t start playing, if you don’t implement this method or return YES, the player starts.
  • – playerDidStopPlaying:

    Called after the player stopped playing. This method is called both when the current song ends and if the user stops the playback.
  • – playerDidStopPlayingLastTrack:

    Called after the player stopped playing the last track.
  • – playerShouldStopPlaying:

    Called before the player stops playing but after the user initiated the stop action. By returning NO here, the delegate may prevent the player from stopping the playback.
  • – player:DidSeekToPosition:

    Called after the player seeked or scrubbed to a new position. This is mostly the result of a user interaction.
  • – playerShouldSkipForward:

    Called before the player actually skips to the next song, but after the user initiated that action.
  • – playerDidSkipForward:

    Called after the player skipped to the next track, but before playback began.
  • – playerShouldSkipBackwards:

    Called before the player actually skips to the previous song, but after the user initiated that action.
  • – playerDidSkipBackwards:

    Called after the player skipped to the previous track, but before playback begins.
  • – player:didChangeVolume:

    Called when the player’s volume changed
